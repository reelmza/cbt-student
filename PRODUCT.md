# Product

## Register

product

## Users

University and tertiary students (Nigerian/African HEIs) taking formal, time-limited CBT exams. They access the portal from a range of devices: campus desktops, personal laptops, and Android phones on variable network connections. They are under time and academic pressure when using most screens. They are not power users; many treat technology as a means to an end. A first-year student who has never sat a computer-based exam is the default mental model.

## Product Purpose

OayasTech CBT-V2 Students is the student-facing exam portal for the OayasTech CBT platform. It handles student login, exam discovery, pre-exam instructions, active exam-taking (objective, theory, and subjective questions), answer auto-save, and submission. Success means a student can log in, start their exam, work through all questions, and submit without confusion or anxiety caused by the interface.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, clear, accessible. OayasTech should feel like a competent institution that respects its students' time and intelligence without being cold or bureaucratic. Not a social app, not enterprise software.

## Anti-references

- **Moodle / Blackboard / legacy CBT systems**: institution-gray, cluttered toolbars, table-soup layouts, no whitespace, dated font choices. The default "CBT software" aesthetic is the thing to escape.
- **Gamified edu-tech (Duolingo, Quizlet)**: too casual and colorful for a formal examination setting.
- **Government portal aesthetic**: table-heavy, zero breathing room, low-contrast text, 2005-era button styles.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm under pressure.** Exam screens must reduce cognitive load, not add to it. Every screen strips away anything that doesn't help the student do their work. Noise is the enemy of performance.
2. **Trust through honesty.** Every status signal — countdown timer, save indicator, question progress — must be visible, immediate, and unambiguous. Students cannot afford to wonder whether their answer was saved.
3. **Accessible by default.** The interface must work on a budget Android phone in bright outdoor light, on a campus desktop with an aging browser, and for students with limited digital fluency. Contrast, touch targets, and font sizes are not negotiables.
4. **Friendly, not casual.** The tone and visual warmth welcome students without undermining the formality of an exam. OayasTech is on the student's side, not above them.
5. **Speed signals respect.** Loading and transition states should feel fast and purposeful. A student mid-exam has no time to wait. Every perceived delay costs trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline.
- Touch targets must be viable on small Android screens (44px minimum).
- Font size 16px minimum on all form inputs (already enforced to prevent iOS zoom).
- Reduced motion: avoid animations that could distract students mid-exam.
- Color should never be the sole indicator of state (answered vs. unanswered questions, error vs. success).
